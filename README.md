# NEXA WORKFORCE ANALYTICS
This project focuses on helping Nexa Ltd gain deeper visibility into its workforce through data. As organizations face increasing challenges around employee engagement, performance management, workforce planning, and training effectiveness, leadership needed a centralized view of key HR metrics to support strategic decision-making.

As the data analyst, I transformed workforce data into an interactive Excel dashboard that provides insights into employee demographics, engagement levels, performance outcomes, training investments, and workforce development initiatives.


---

## 🎯 Project Overview

The goal of this project is to transform workforce data into a visually interactive dashboard that helps business stakeholders:

- Understand workforce demographics and employee distribution
- Monitor employee engagement, satisfaction, and performance metrics
- Track training participation, completion, and investment outcomes
- Identify succession planning and workforce renewal risks
- Support data-driven talent management and workforce planning decisions

## 📈 Key Insights

- **Workforce Overview:** Nexa employs 2,845 employees, with 56% female and 44% male representation. The workforce has an average age of 49 years and an average tenure of 5 years. The largest employee segment falls within the 55+ age group, highlighting a potential succession planning challenge as a significant portion of experienced employees approach retirement.

  <img width="998" height="505" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/c1695c1f-5a47-4e02-a65e-25eb957489ae" />

- **Department & Workforce Distribution:** The Production department represents the largest workforce segment, accounting for over half of the company's employees. This concentration increases operational dependence on a single department and emphasizes the need for workforce balancing and talent pipeline development across other business functions.

- **Employee Engagement & Performance:** Employee sentiment is moderate, with average scores of:

Engagement Score: 2.9
Satisfaction Score: 3.0
Work-Life Balance Score: 3.0
Employee Rating: 3.0

A total of 346 employees (12.16%) were identified as high performers. Most employees fall within the "Fully Meets Expectations" performance category, indicating stable but largely average performance across the organization.

<img width="1017" height="505" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/3cff0f21-4ce7-4eb4-8071-2b86c54efc89" />

- **Training & Development:** Nexa invested approximately $1.59M in workforce development initiatives across 2,845 training programs. Training completion stands at 50.83%, with an average training duration of 3 days.

The Production department received the largest share of training investment ($1.07M), significantly exceeding investment levels in other departments.

-**Training Effectiveness:** While participation levels remain strong across training programs, completion and outcome metrics suggest that increased investment does not always translate into improved effectiveness. Internal training programs showed higher completion rates but also recorded notable failed and incomplete outcomes, indicating opportunities for training quality improvements.

## 💡 Recommendations
### 1) Succession Planning & Workforce Renewal The large concentration of employees within the 55+ age group presents a potential retirement risk. 
- **Actions:**
   - Develop succession plans for critical roles.
   - Launch mentorship and knowledge-transfer programs
   - Strengthen graduate and early-career recruitment pipelines. 
- **KPIs:**
  - 100% succession coverage for critical positions. 
  - 20% increase in early-career talent hiring.

### 2) Employee Engagement Improvement Moderate engagement and satisfaction scores indicate opportunities for workforce improvement. 
- **Actions:**
   - Conduct regular employee feedback surveys.
   - Implement recognition and career development programs.
   - Strengthen manager-employee communication channels. 
- **KPIs:**
  - Increase engagement score from 2.9 to 3.5+.
  - Improve employee satisfaction by 15%. 

### 3) Performance Development Strategy Most employees fall within the "Fully Meets Expectations" category, with limited representation among top performers. 
- **Actions:**
  - Introduce targeted development plans for high-potential employees.
  - Expand leadership development programs.
  - Establish performance coaching initiatives.
- **KPIs:**
  - Increase high performer population by 20%.
  - Improve average employee rating to 3.5+.
  
### 4) Training ROI Optimization Training investment should be tied more closely to measurable performance outcomes
- **Actions:**
  - Introduce post-training assessments and ROI tracking.
  - Review low-performing training programs.
  - Prioritize high-impact learning initiatives.
- **KPIs:** - Increase training completion rate above 70%.
  - Reduce failed training outcomes by 25%.
### 5) Workforce Distribution Optimization Heavy workforce concentration within Production creates operational dependency. 
- **Actions:** - Improve workforce planning across departments.
  - Build talent pipelines in underrepresented business functions.
  - Monitor departmental staffing needs regularly.
- **KPIs:**
  - Reduce workforce concentration risk.
  - Improve staffing balance across business units.
